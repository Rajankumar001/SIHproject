About Judiciary

  1. The judiciary acts as the guardian of the Constitution, ensuring that any amendments to it follow the prescribed procedures and do not violate its basic structure.
  2. Court judgments set legal precedents, which guide future decisions and help in maintaining consistency and predictability in the legal system.
  3. Upholding the public's trust in the judicial system by promoting transparency, integrity, and accountability among judges and court personnel.
